1. Foundations of AI
   
- Definition of AI
Answer :
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science and technology that enables machines, computers, or software to simulate human intelligence, including learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and decision-making. These systems analyze data to perform complex, often automated, tasks such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, and generating content.

- Types of AI (ANI, AGI, ASI)

  Answer:

Artificial Intelligence is categorized into three stages based on capability: ANI (Narrow/Weak AI) for specialized tasks, AGI (General/Strong AI) for human-level intelligence, and ASI (Superintelligence) for surpassing human capabilities. Currently, all existing AI is ANI; AGI and ASI are theoretical, future advancements representing a, b, and c.

The Three Main Types of AI
Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI): Often called "Weak AI," ANI is specialized to perform specific tasks, such as facial recognition, language translation, or internet searches. These systems, including ChatGPT or Siri, operate under pre-determined constraints and cannot operate outside their domain.
Artificial General Intelligence (AGI): Also known as "Strong AI," AGI would be a machine that possesses the ability to understand, learn, and apply knowledge across any intellectual task just like a human. It is characterized by adaptability and the ability to generalize knowledge.
Artificial Superintelligence (ASI): This theoretical level refers to AI that surpasses human intelligence across all fields, including creativity, emotional intelligence, and problem-solving. ASI is generally depicted as an AI that can improve itself recursively, far exceeding human intellect.

Comparison of AI Types
Intelligence Level: ANI has limited, task-specific intelligence. AGI matches human intelligence. ASI surpasses human intelligence.
Adaptability: ANI is rigid, whereas AGI has high adaptability, and ASI has extreme, superior adaptability.
Autonomy: ANI requires human oversight. AGI is expected to work independently. ASI could act completely on its own.

- Turing Test
- Intelligent Agents
Answer :
Intelligent agents are autonomous software entities that perceive their environment, process information, and take independent actions to achieve specific goals. Using sensors and actuators, they learn from data, make decisions, and adapt to dynamic scenarios, ranging from simple rule-based automation to complex AI systems like Siri.
Key Characteristics and Components
Perception: They use sensors (e.g., microphones, Cameras, APIs) to gather data from their environment.
Autonomy: They operate without constant human supervision.
Goal-Oriented: They act to achieve specific, predefined outcomes.
Adaptability: They learn from past experiences and data to improve future performance
